About Santa Ana, CA

Santa Ana, the county seat of Orange County, is a diverse city in California known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and strong community. As of the 2022 census, the population of Santa Ana was 308,189, making it the second-most populous city in Orange County and the second-largest city in the Greater Los Angeles area. The city is home to several points of interest, including the Santa Ana Zoo, the Discovery Cube, and the historic downtown area. The median household income in Santa Ana is $84,210, and the median property value is $719,800.

The largest ethnic groups in Santa Ana are White (Non-Hispanic) (44.5%), Hispanic (38.5%), and Asian (Non-Hispanic) (9.5%). The city has a diverse economy, with a focus on healthcare, education, and retail. The average commute time in Santa Ana is 25.0 minutes, and most residents drive alone to work.

The latest development in Santa Ana includes the construction of new housing units to address the housing shortage in the area. The city is also investing in transportation infrastructure to improve traffic flow and reduce congestion. The latitude and longitude of Santa Ana are 33.7879° N, 117.8531° W. Overall, Santa Ana is a vibrant and diverse community with a strong economy, rich history, and a variety of attractions.
