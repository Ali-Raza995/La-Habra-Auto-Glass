About Orange, CA

Orange is a charming city in Orange County, California, known for its historic charm and strong community. With a population of 139,911 as of 2020, the city's demographics are diverse, with the largest ethnic group being White (Non-Hispanic) at 39%, followed by Asian (Non-Hispanic) at 21.1%, and White (Hispanic) at 14.6%. The median household income in 2021 was $100,485, reflecting a strong spending capacity among its residents.

Orange is home to several points of interest, including the Santa Ana River, the Richard Nixon Presidential Library and Museum, and the renowned University of California, Irvine. The city's coordinates are approximately 33.6370° N latitude and 117.9142° W longitude. The latest development about the city includes the ongoing efforts to revitalize the downtown area, with a focus on preserving the city's historic architecture and promoting local businesses.
