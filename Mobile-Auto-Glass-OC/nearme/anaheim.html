About Anaheim, CA

Anaheim, located in northern Orange County, California, is a vibrant city with a rich history and diverse population. As of 2023, Anaheim has a population of 343,654, with an average household income of $103,011 and a poverty rate of 13.04%. The city's economy has been thriving, with a focus on tourism and entertainment, driven in part by the presence of Disneyland and other attractions. The median age in Anaheim is 35.3 years, and the city has a diverse demographic makeup, with a significant Hispanic population.

In terms of points of interest, Anaheim is home to the world-famous Disneyland Resort, which has been a major driver of the city's economy and a significant draw for tourists from around the world. Other attractions include the Anaheim Convention Center, Angel Stadium, and the Honda Center, offering a wide range of entertainment and sporting events.

The city's latest development includes ongoing efforts to address housing needs and population growth. Anaheim's population is projected to grow, and the city is working to achieve a balanced housing stock that meets the varied needs of its residents. Additionally, the city has been investing in transportation infrastructure to support its growing population and improve connectivity.

The latitude and longitude of Anaheim are 33.8366° N, 117.9143° W. Overall, Anaheim is a dynamic city with a strong economy, diverse population, and a wide array of attractions, making it a significant cultural and economic hub in Southern California.
