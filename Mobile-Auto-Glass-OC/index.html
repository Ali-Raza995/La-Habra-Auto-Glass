About Irvine, CA

Irvine is a master-planned city in southern Orange County, California, United States, in the Los Angeles metropolitan area. As of the 2020 census, the city had a population of 307,670, making it the 63rd most populous city in the United States. The city is known for its clean and well-maintained environment, pleasant living atmosphere, and strong sense of community. The median household income in Irvine is $114,027, and the median property value is $900,700.

The city is home to the University of California, Irvine (UCI), which is the largest university in the city and has been recognized for its top-notch schools and universities. Other notable educational institutions in Irvine include Irvine Valley College and the University of Massachusetts Global.

Irvine's economy is diverse, with a focus on technology and semiconductor sectors. The city is home to several large corporations, particularly in these industries. The average commute time in Irvine is 25.1 minutes, and most residents drive alone to work.

In terms of demographics, the largest ethnic groups in Irvine are Asian (Non-Hispanic) (42.6%), White (Non-Hispanic) (37.4%), Two+ (Non-Hispanic) (5.61%), White (Hispanic) (5.57%), and Two+ (Hispanic) (3.33%). The city consistently ranks as one of the safest in the United States, with a low rate of registered sex offenders.

Some points of interest in Irvine include the Orange County Great Park, the Irvine Museum, and the California Science Center. The city also hosts various events and gatherings that showcase its strong sense of community
