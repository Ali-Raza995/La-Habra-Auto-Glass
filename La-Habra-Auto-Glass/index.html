About La Habra, California

La Habra, located in the northwest corner of Orange County, California, is a city rich in history and community spirit. Originally known as "Pass Through the Hills," a name given by Spanish explorers in 1769, La Habra was incorporated in 1925 and has since grown into a suburban area with a population of over 60,000. It's known for its historical ties to the avocado industry, particularly the popular 'Hass' avocado, which originated here.

The city boasts a diverse community, with a significant Hispanic or Latino population. La Habra offers more affordable housing options compared to other Southern California areas, with a median household income of $60,954. Its semi-walkable layout, with a walk score of 61, allows residents to balance pedestrian activities with the convenience of car travel for longer distances.

La Habra operates under a council-manager government system and is served by multiple school districts, including the La Habra City School District. The city is well-connected despite lacking freeways, thanks to three state highways.

Community life in La Habra is vibrant, highlighted by the annual Corn Festival, which celebrates the city's agricultural heritage and fosters a strong sense of community. The city's commitment to parks, community services, and events underscores its family-friendly and culturally diverse character.
