About Orange, CA

Orange, CA is a city located in Southern California, approximately 31 miles southeast of Los Angeles. As of the 2020 census, the population of Orange was 139,857, with a median age of 36 and a median household income of $102,125. The city is known for its charming downtown area, which features historic buildings, shops, and restaurants, as well as its close proximity to popular attractions such as Disneyland Resort and the beach communities of Orange County. The demographic of Orange is diverse, with the following ethnic groups: White (Non-Hispanic) (41.5%), White (Hispanic) (20.8%), Asian (Non-Hispanic) (12.9%), Other (Hispanic) (11.3%), and Two+ (Hispanic) (6.17%).

The city is home to several higher educational institutions, including Chapman University, which is known for its undergraduate and law programs. Some of the points of

interest in Orange include the Hilbert Museum of California Art, Orange Circle Antique Mall, Leatherby Libraries, Eisenhower Park, Steve Ambriz Memorial Park, Plaza Park, Hart Park, and Christmas Lights In Orange County. The city is also known for its beautiful neighborhoods, such as El Modena, North El Modena, Orange Park Acres, and Olive, which still have unincorporated portions of land that have not yet been annexed.

The latitude and longitude coordinates for Orange, CA are 33.787914° N, -117.853104° W. The city is located in the Pacific Time Zone, observing Daylight Saving Time during the summer months
