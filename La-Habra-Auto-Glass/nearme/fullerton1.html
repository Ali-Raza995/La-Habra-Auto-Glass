About Fullerton, CA

Fullerton, CA is a city located in northern Orange County, California, United States. As of the 2020 census, the city had a total population of 143,617, with a median age of 35.4 and a median household income of $92,718.

Fullerton was founded in 1887 and is home to numerous higher educational institutions, particularly California State University, Fullerton and Fullerton College. Historically, it was a center of agriculture, notably groves of Valencia oranges and other citrus crops, petroleum extraction, transportation, and manufacturing.

The demographic of Fullerton is diverse, with the following ethnic groups: White (Non-Hispanic) (32.2%), Asian (Non-Hispanic) (23.9%), White (Hispanic) (19.1%), Other (Hispanic) (11%), and Two+ (Hispanic) (7.54%). None of the households

Fullerton is home to several points of interest, including the Richard Nixon Presidential Library and Museum, which offers exhibits on the life and presidency of former U.S. President Richard Nixon.
